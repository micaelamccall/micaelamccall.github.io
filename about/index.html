The broad brushstrokes...

  • I am originally from Albuquerque, New Mexico.

  • I migrated from the desert to the swamp to attend Emory University in Atlanta, graduating in 2018 with a B.S. in Neuroscience and a B.A. in Religious Studies.

  • Then, I traded in the swamp and bought my first-ever winter jacket, migrating north to join the National Institutes of Health as a post-baccalaureate research fellow.

Research & data science

  • I was drawn to neuroscience research by the excitement of exploring complex data to uncover pertinent findings on groundbreaking topics.

  • I quickly found that above all, the most thrilling part of research for me is the process of transforming a pile of complex data into communicable insights.

My work...

  • In my fellowship at the NIH, I have learned to create data processing pipelines, apply statistical analyses, and create visualizations to communicate discoveries from physiological, behavioral, and clinical data.
  • Through immersion in data science classes, books, articles, and coding practice, I have learned how to understand and implement ML algorithms.
  • My experience doing anthropological field work, behavioral research with Rhesus macaques, and clinical neuroscience research in humans has enriched my perspective and taught me how to communicate and collaborate with diverse audiences.

I am currently seeking career opportunities

  • I am particularly interested in progressive causes related to mental health, healthcare, environmental protection, and social justice.
  • I derive energy from dynamic and rapidly-changing fields.
  • I have the ability to learn new skills quickly and utilize all available resources to produce high-quality work.

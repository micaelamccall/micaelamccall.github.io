About me

I am originally from Albuquerque, New Mexico. I migrated from the desert to the swamp to attend Emory University in Atlanta, graduating in 2018 with a B.S. in Neuroscience and a B.A. in Religious Studies.

Then, I traded in the swamp and bought my first-ever winter jacket, migrating north to join the NIH as a post-baccalaureate research fellow. I was drawn to neuroscience research by the excitement of exploring complex data to uncover pertinent findings on groundbreaking topics.

I quickly found that above all, the most thrilling part of research for me is the process of transforming a pile of complex data into communicable insights.

In my current position, I create data processing pipelines, apply statistical analyses, and create visualizations to communicate discoveries from physiological, behavioral, and clinical data.

I am currently seeking opportunities to grow into the role of a data scientist.

My plug for myself/my work style in a nutshell:

  • I am motivated by the idea that data-driven understandings of human behavior, systems, and society can be ethically applied to make people's lives better.
  • I derive energy from dynamic and rapidly-changing fields.
  • I have the ability to learn new skills quickly and utilize all available resources to produce high-quality work.
